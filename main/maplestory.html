Introduction to Maple Story

Maple Story is a free-to-play, 2D, side-scrolling massive multiplayer online role-playing game, developed by the South Korean company Wizet. Several version of the game are available for specific countries or regions, and each is published by various companies such as Nexon. Although one may play the game for free, one can only purchase some character appearances and gameplay enhancements from the Cash Shop with real money.

In the game, players travel the "Maple World", defeating monsters and developing their characters' skills and abilities as is typical in role-playing games. Players can interact with others in many ways, such as through chatting, trading and playing minigames. Groups of players can band together in parties to hunt monsters and share the rewards and can also collaborate forming guilds to interact with each other.

Casual & Easy-going Commnity

Unlike Ragnarok Online where the players are very competitive, Maple Story is more casual and focused on social and exploration aesthetics. The difference in what the community wants made me focus on a creating new content and world events rather than skill design. Here's a summary of the changes and new content that I've introduced to the game:

  • Redesigned the level progresssion: It is no joke when I say it can take months to reach max level (200) even if you're a hardcore player. By tweaking the leveling system, players have more freedom to explore new areas every day instead of every week.
  • City invasion: Nothing makes the game experience more exciting than having a randomized events in your game whether it is programmed or player-controlled events. This is especially true for social type games. With proper planning and calculations, it would create an unforgettable experience for both low level and high level players.
  • Secret and Legendary Quests: To expand on the exploration aesthetic of the game, I've added new quests that serves as a fun factor while filling in some of the unused space in the original game.

Conclusion

Unlike Ragnarok Online private server, I did not add or alter the game design as much. The community was extremely satisfied with where the game was so I decided to focus more on upgrading servers, fixing bugs and exploits that might ruin the game balance. I have the to say, MapleCraze community was extremely friendly and fun to play with. The countless nights staying up sqaushing bugs was definitely worth it.
