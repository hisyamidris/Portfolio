Introduction to Wrinkle Wrumble

Wrinkle Wrumble is a multiplayer action game in which multiple number of players battle each other using various props such as chairs, vase, ball, or anything at all in the room. The design was inspired by one of my favorite Playstation 1 game, Poy Poy 2. I teamed up with Sahle Alturaigi, who was also a software engineering student, to develop game in less than a month.

Design and Challenges

Unlike Emergency Room Game (another game I designed using Unity3D) where I focused on the main aesthetic to deliver to my players, I approached this game by thinking of the mechanics first. Although this is a one-month project for "Video Game design and Production" course in University of Colorado - Boulder, we took on the challenge of making it a multiplayer game.

In that short period of time, we learned a lot about both game design and programming:

  • Beware of scope creep!: It is easy to get caught adding another "something super awesome" to your game and losing track of the true purpose of the game and how much time you have to develop it. We have to keep our mind disciplined by defining what the main components of the game should be. We completed the main component of what make the game fun to play first before thinking about adding other features.
  • Balance complexity and depth: The lesser the rule, data and calculation players has to make, the easier it is for them to start enjoying your game. This is where tutorials and UI design comes is important. For a simple game like Wrinkle Wrumble, the controls and ui should be more intuitive.
  • Meaningful choices: If you've played the game, you would realize that most good objects are located near the center of the stage. This forces the player to make a choice between risking yourself being exposed at the middle of the stage or take the opportunity to damage the player who decide to take that risk.

Conclusion

This is my very first multiplayer game that I'm really proud of. It is obvious that there's a lot of features that were lacking and definitely needed some impovements, but I learned a lot from the experience of making it from scratch. It taught me a lot on balancing games, how multiplayer game programming is done and how to successfully manage a game project.

I've also uploaded Wrinkle Wrumble so that you too can play it and experience it yourself! Though keep in mind that the game was made on a beta build of Unity3D so it may be buggy on newer browsers.
